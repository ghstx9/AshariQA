Aqidah (Creed)

The fundamental beliefs that form the foundation of Islamic faith, including belief in God, His attributes, the prophets, the Day of Judgment, and divine decree. Aqidah provides the philosophical framework for understanding existence and purpose.

Reason and Revelation

Islam emphasizes the harmony between reason ('aql) and revelation (wahy). While revelation provides absolute truth, reason serves as a tool to understand and apply this truth in various contexts of human experience.

Divine Justice

The concept of divine justice (adl) is central to Islamic philosophy. God's justice is perfect and encompasses both mercy and wisdom, providing the foundation for moral and ethical frameworks in Islamic thought.

Purpose and Meaning

Islamic philosophy addresses the fundamental question of human purpose through the concept of 'ibadah (worship) and khilafah (stewardship). Life has inherent meaning through submission to God and fulfilling our role as trustees of creation.

The Nature of Existence

Islamic philosophy addresses fundamental questions about the nature of existence, the purpose of creation, and humanity's role in the universe. The concept of Tawhid (monotheism) is not just a theological principle but a comprehensive worldview that influences every aspect of philosophical inquiry.

The relationship between the Creator and creation is understood through various philosophical frameworks, including discussions on causality, free will versus divine decree (qadar), and the nature of time and eternity. These discussions have shaped Islamic thought for centuries and continue to provide insights for contemporary philosophical discourse.
